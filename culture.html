Cultural Highlights

Fado is the soulful music genre that embodies the essence of Portuguese culture. Originating in the early 19th century in Lisbon, Fado expresses themes of longing, love, and the sea, reflecting Portugal’s maritime heritage. Sung with deep emotion, Fado is typically accompanied by the Portuguese guitar and a classical guitar, with lyrics that tell tales of life’s sorrows and joys.

Portuguese festivals are vibrant, joyful celebrations that showcase the country’s deep-rooted traditions. From the lively Carnival in February to the Feast of São João in Porto, there is always a reason to celebrate. In June, Lisbon comes alive with the Festas de Lisboa, where streets are decorated, parades fill the avenues, and people feast on sardines.

Portugal has a rich tradition of artisanal crafts, passed down through generations, reflecting the nation’s history and creativity. From the famous azulejos—intricately painted ceramic tiles—to woven textiles and fine pottery, Portuguese craftsmanship is a blend of artistry and heritage. In cities like Coimbra and Lisbon, you can find workshops that create handmade ceramic pieces.
