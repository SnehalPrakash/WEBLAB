Must-Visit Places

Lisbon

Lisbon, the vibrant capital of Portugal, is a city where old-world charm meets a bustling, modern atmosphere. Its famous yellow trams wind through narrow, cobbled streets that showcase picturesque buildings covered in colorful tiles, known as azulejos. Lisbon’s iconic landmarks, like the Belém Tower and the Jerónimos Monastery, reflect Portugal’s storied maritime history.

Porto

Porto, Portugal’s second-largest city, is known for its rich history, unique architecture, and, of course, its world-famous Port wine. Located on the banks of the Douro River, Porto boasts a stunning mix of medieval, baroque, and contemporary architecture, with the Dom Luís I Bridge as its most famous landmark. The city’s Ribeira district, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is a maze of winding streets lined with brightly painted houses and quaint cafes..

Algarve

The Algarve is Portugal’s sun-soaked southern region, famous for its dramatic cliffs, golden beaches, and hidden coves. With a landscape that ranges from rugged coastlines to serene fishing villages, it’s a haven for beach lovers, surfers, and hikers. Popular spots like Lagos, Albufeira, and Faro offer stunning coastal views, while the Ponta da Piedade rock formations are a sight to behold.
